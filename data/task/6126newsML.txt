The Mexican stock exchange said Wednesday that shares for Compania Industrial de San Cristobal (Cristoba) have been delisted following Cristoba's merger with Kimberly Clark de Mexico.
In March, Kimberly Clark bought out Cristoba's existing shares by offering 0.57265 of Kimberly's shares for each Cristoba share.
The move followed the international merger of paper giants Kimberly Clark Corp and  Scott Paper Co, which was the parent company of Cristoba.
