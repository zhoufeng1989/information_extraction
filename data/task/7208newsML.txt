Futuris Corp Ltd said it would not raise its bid for rural house Elders Australia Ltd.
An independent expert had found the offer to be fair and reasonable and it had been well supported by Elders' shareholders, the company said in a statement to the Australian Stock Exchange.
Nearly 50 percent of Elders' shareholders had accepted the offer and it was now entitled to about 42 percent of the target's issued capital, Futuris said.
Futuris is offering 10 of its shares for every nine Elders shares plus 23 cents per Elders share.  
The offer for Elders is due to close at 5.00 p.m. (0900 GMT) on September 2.
Based on current prices, the bid values Elders at about A$1.77 per share.
At 2.50 p.m. (0450 GMT) Elders shares were unchanged at A$1.73 on turnover of 128,812 shares while Futuris shares were also unchanged at A$1.39 on turnover of 81,567 shares.
