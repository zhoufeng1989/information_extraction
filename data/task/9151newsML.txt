Hungarian Telephone and Cable Corp (HTCC) has transferred all its senior management activities from the U.S. to Hungary, HTCC said in a statement.
Certain senior officials of HTCC were replaced in connection with the transfer, the statement said, adding that James G. Morrison, former Vice President and Chief Operating Officer in Budapest, has been elected as President and Chief Executive Officer.
HTCC is the largest local telephone concession operator in Hungary. It holds five of Hungary's 17 local telephone concessions and it has declared that if the opportunity arose it would consider acquiring other concessions.
HTCC's largest single shareholder is the U.S.'s Citizens' Utilities, which holds a 19.9 percent stake and has options to buy up to 54 percent.
