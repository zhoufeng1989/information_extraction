The share price of trading and telecommunication company Transmarco Ltd shot up at the open on Wednesday after news that Indonesian tobacco tycoon Putera Sampoearna had raised his takeover bid for the company.
At 0118 GMT, Transmarco jumped S$1.10 or more than 11 percent to S$10.90 on volume of 1.67 million shares, after hitting an early high of S$11.90.  
Standard Chartered Merchant Bank Asia and Swiss Bank Corp said on Tuesday that Putera Sampoerna, through his investment company Miel Investment Corp, had raised his bid for Transmarco to S$8.55 per share from S$6.50.
"We could see the shares hitting S$12 soon," said a dealer.
