The Brazilian unit of U.S. car maker General Motors Corp expects to end 1996 with sales of at least $700 million, some 150 percent above its 1995 performance, a local news agency reported.
Citing GM do Brasil director Luiz Moan Yabiko Junior, the private Invest News agency also said the company hoped to have exported $3.6 billion from now till the end of 1999.
Yabiko Junior was attending a seminar in Brasilia on the metal industry, hosted by the National Industry Confederation.
